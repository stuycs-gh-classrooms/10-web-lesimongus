How to Rocket Jump

In Team Fortress 2, the Soldier's primary weapon is a Rocket Launcher, which shoots a projectile that deals good damage, has splash, and will cause knockback. As one might expect from an explosive weapon, this projectile will deal splash damage to the player, albeit much less damage than what an enemy would have taken, if the player is too close to what it hits. This is where the fun begins, however, as intentionally hitting oneself with the rocket's explosion and adding a few steps opens the way for the most fun feature in the game: Rocket Jumping. Rocket Jumping, at its basics, is fairly simple.

  • Look at any nearby surface like a wall or the ground, somewhere that would catch the player in the explosion if a rocket is shot there. Do note that where you shoot the rocket will affect your trajectory and speed.
  • Crouch, jump, and shoot all at once. Or in other words, press left ctrl (many players rebind crouch to shift), space, and mouse1 at the same time. Though crouching is optional, it will let you get much higher heights.
  • If all has been done correctly, you should now be hurtling through the air at high speeds. What you do now that you are airborne is up to you, whether it's to rocket jump again off a wall or shoot your rockets at the enemy.

The Purposes of Rocket Jumping

Now that we have established how to do this, we now have to go over what this can be used for. Rocket jumping is a fun movement technique that allows the second slowest class in the game to reach incredible speeds and heights that no other class can get to. In higher play, rocket jumping becomes an integral part of Soldier, being so impactful that in the 6v6 competitive format there are two Soldiers in each team, one Pocket Soldier who mostly sticks with the Medic and the rest of the combo, and one Roamer, who plays more offensively and will often rocket jump to take out an important enemy player, often the Medic. The main reasons players rocket jump are to:

  • Get to higher places, allowing the Soldier to see from a better vantage point as well as fight better due to being on the high ground.
  • Get to places faster. Rocket jumping preserves all momentum, so when you chain Rocket Jumps together, you can get to locations extremely fast.
  • On a similar note to the previous point, this allows Soldier to go from Spawn to the main fighting area extremely fast. Soldiers will often be one of the first to get to the fight, which is crucial as they are great at dealing damage and making space. This is an example of a good rollout, and connects to the next point.
  • Get the jump on enemies and take them down quickly, or chase them down. What you saw in the previous clip was an example of the former. In normal 6s matches on Process (the map in the video), the Soldier is generally on one of the crates in the middle, which gives them high ground to shoot from and call out enemy positions. However, the Soldier in the video altered the rollout to go fast enough to meet the enemy team as they are just about to enter the midpoint. This is way farther than where the Soldier generally should be, which catches the enemy team off guard and allows the Soldier to take down the Medic, leaving the enemy team to have to fight mid without healing, which is extremely disadvantage and many teams will often just retreat to defend second point if their Medic dies too early. In the latter case, Soldiers will rocket jump towards a finishing enemy to finish them off, as the Soldier's rockets deal more damage the closer the Soldier is to the target.
  • Use the Market Gardener on enemies. The Market Gardener is an unlockable melee weapon that deals guaranteed critical hits (3x damage) on players it hits, on the condition that the Soldier has to be airborne from a rocket jump to do so. Some players will dedicate their entire playstyle around this, and are commonly dubbed as Trolldiers by the community, because although the playstyle tends to be impractical, players will be very annoyed if they actually do get taken down by a Trolldier.

Why It's So Cool

So, the character gets a nice movement technique, what of it? There are plenty of games that contain fun movement mechanics. Soldier isn't the only one who can rocket jump either; Demoman's stickies have a similar effect if he tries the same procedure. However, rocket jumping stands out among the sea of cool movement mechanics in video games for a variety of reasons.

  • Despite being a bit more simple when compared to other forms of movement in other games, rocket jumping can lead to a plethora of different interactions. Where you shoot matters, and what situation you're in can drastically change what you should do. The player can shoot straight down to gain a lot of vertical height, shoot off of a wall or away from them for better speed, or jump off a wall midair for more time in the air. This is what separates rocket jumping from sticky jumping, as Demoman can only really benefit from two stickies before he takes himself out, when even one sticky can get him low enough that he shouldn't engage in the fight.
  • It's risky. The rockets can take large chunks off of the Soldier's health, and it's common to see some players get very close to a Soldier that they're fighting to make them take recoil damage. With rocket jumping, this makes each jump count, causes the Soldier to think about when they should jump, and ultimately makes the moments where they succeed that much more thrilling and satisfying. Even the Gunboats, which lets you take less self-damage has a risk, as it causes you to lose out on the benefits on another weapon in your secondary slot.
  • It can define a lot of match-ups. A Soldier that isn't good at rocket jumping is significantly worse than a Soldier that can rocket jump but is otherwise at a similar skill level, since they simply can't close the gap between faster classes like Scout or get the jump on vital classes like Demoman, Medic, and Sniper. The aforementioned Gunboats also causes you to play more carefully around Pyros, because their ability to deflect your rockets is much more impactful when you can't switch to a shotgun, which they can't deflect.
  • It's just fun. It's very satisfying to zoom across the map at light speed to send a particularly annoying Sniper to the shadow realm with a shovel, to suddenly appear when your enemy wasn't expecting it and take out their med, or to just jump around the map. A bunch of other players share this sentiment, and there are entire community servers dedicated to help players get better at rocket jumping or to test how fast a skilled rocket jumper can clear a course.
